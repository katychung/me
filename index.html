Pangram - Preserving Minority Languages

Pangram is a social network for reviving and preserving minority languages. As spoken word comes before literacy (yet it is often the most ignored element) the site aims to provide an intriguing audio based platform that encourages the less fluent to connect with native speakers while adding to a growing archive of spoken word.

Everybody - Interactive Installation

A solo, one semester 3rd year project experimenting with Processing, PHP, MySQL, HTML and CSS. The user types in an (anonymous) altruistic message, messages are stored and over time they are projected onto a surface for passers-by to view.

I used Processing (Java) and MySQL for this project. The idea was inspired by Postsecret and Japanese religion Shinto and how they would hang anonymous prayers/wishes in the shrines to be read by the Gods.

Objects for Grandpeople (Microsoft brief)

In a team of four, we created the networked bowl. Andrew's grandparent Frances had an avid interest in baking, the problem however was that Andrew and Frances did not see each other often as they lived far apart. If Andrew wanted a recipe, Frances would offer a vague description of the recipe e.g. she might have said "a pinch of...". The networked bowl enabled Frances to pass Andrew baking recipes; the object displayed an indicator informing Andrew of the quantity alongside a recording of Frances' spoken instruction.
